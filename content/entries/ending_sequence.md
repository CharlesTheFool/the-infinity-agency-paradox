**SOLANUM**: "Look up."

You turn your gaze to the sky. The sun has swollen to impossible size, its surface roiling with violent plasma storms. The end is near.

**SOLANUM**: "The sun is dying. Soon it will collapse, then explode, scattering every atom of every planet across existence. Nothing will remain.

Except..."

She gestures to the inscriptions around you, the Nomai texts you've spent hours (loops) deciphering.

**SOLANUM**: "Except knowledge. Except understanding. These words will evaporate, but what they taught you... that persists.

You traveled this solar system. You could visit locations in any order. You could read texts in any sequence. And yet..."

The sun flares.

Light floods the Quantum Moon.

**SOLANUM**: "...you arrived at the same truth. The same synthesis.

Not because your actions were predetermined, but because your understanding converged on reality.

This is epistemic agency. Not the power to change outcomes but the power to navigate knowledge. The power to choose how you arrive at truth."

## The Categorical Error

Game discourse conflates agency with outcome control. We measure meaningful choice by counting branches, endings, consequence variance.

This framework blinds us to games that create agency through epistemic navigation. control over understanding pathways rather than narrative outcomes.

---

## The Provocation

*Life is Strange*: 40 endings, low epistemic dimensionality
*Outer Wilds*: 1 ending, infinite epistemic dimensionality

Which has "more agency"?

The answer depends on how we measure possibility space.
